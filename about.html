About Stacks

Berkeley began as an offshoot of San Francisco, an intimate neighbor to industrial Oakland, and, of course, a college town. Home to the University of California, the city has made a name for itself as a hotbed of intellectual thought—from free speech to nuclear power—and subcultural social movements.

The Daily Californian serves as a source of knowledge for UC Berkeley’s students, informing readers of the city’s goings-on and contextualizing the confusion and clarity of daily campus life. We are the city of Berkeley’s paper of record and only remaining pluralistic public forum.

Stacks looks deeper into the swirling maze of innovation and speculation that underpins our campus and our city. From this name, our mission is derived. Main Stacks: a place synonymous with the Berkeley experience in its magnitude, knowledge, and chaotic energy.

In Stacks, we aim to unravel all realms of life—from aspiring baristas and future dropouts-turned-CEOs to red-eyed engineers, beatniks, and fashionistas. Stacks is layered but not static; at each level, know nothing and know everything. Stacks is your guide to Berkeley as it is, as we think it is, and as we want it to be.

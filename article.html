A Headline That Carries

A short dek that frames the story in two lines at most.

By Firstname Lastname · Aug 29, 2025

Opening graf: set the stakes, the place, the rhythm. Keep sentences varied, keep the verbs working. Readers decide in the first paragraph whether to stay.

Second graf: what’s new here; why this matters now. Provide the context without burying the lede. Quote a voice that grounds the reporting, or present a crisp fact that reframes the obvious.

“Pull the line that hums and trims the rest,” an editor once said. Good advice for writing; better advice for living.

Mid-story: add texture, specifics, names, and numbers. Show the tradeoffs, the timeline, what comes next. Keep paragraphs breathable on mobile.

Close with consequence or return to the original image. Let the ending feel earned, not stapled on.
